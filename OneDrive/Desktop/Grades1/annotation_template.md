## QUICK REFERENCE: KEY LANGUAGE MARKERS

### THREAT FRAME INDICATORS
**Watch for:** threat, risk, danger, vulnerable, jeopardy, crisis, emergency, harm, loss, damage, collapse, decline, job losses, displacement, decline, pressure, squeeze, precarious, deficit, deficit, downturn, recession, bankruptcy, closure, exposure, vulnerable, endangered, at risk, threatened

**Example:** "Trade war threatens manufacturing jobs and risks recession"

### BENEFIT FRAME INDICATORS
**Watch for:** growth, opportunity, expansion, create/creation, jobs, gains, improve/improvement, strengthen, advantage, benefit, prosper, thrive, profit, efficiency, productive, success, advance, enhance, opportunities, prosperity, better, promising, competitive advantage

**Example:** "New policy creates opportunities and expands market access"

### DECISION REMINDERS
1. **Threat AND Benefit can BOTH be YES** – analyze independently
2. **Descriptive only = NO for both** – need explicit framing, not just reporting
3. **Hedged language counts** – "could threaten" = threat frame
4. **Be literal** – explicit language in text, not inferred meaning
5. **Dual justification always** – explain both sides of the determination

## COMMON ANNOTATION PATTERNS

### Pattern 1: CLEAR THREAT (YES/NO)
```
Paragraph: "The policy will eliminate 5,000 manufacturing jobs."
Threat: YES - Explicit job loss language
Benefit: NO - No positive framing present
```

### Pattern 2: CLEAR BENEFIT (NO/YES)
```
Paragraph: "This initiative creates new market opportunities and green jobs."
Threat: NO - No risk or harm language
Benefit: YES - Explicit opportunity and job creation language
```

### Pattern 3: MIXED FRAME (YES/YES)
```
Paragraph: "While some jobs are lost due to automation, overall productivity 
           increases and new roles emerge in tech sectors."
Threat: YES - Job loss explicitly mentioned
Benefit: YES - Productivity increase and job creation mentioned
```

### Pattern 4: NEUTRAL/DESCRIPTIVE (NO/NO)
```
Paragraph: "The economy grew 2.5% last quarter compared to 1.8% the quarter prior."
Threat: NO - Pure reporting, no framing
Benefit: NO - Descriptive data, no explicit benefit framing ("growth" as label, not framed)
```

### Pattern 5: HARD CASE (REQUIRES NOTE)
```
Paragraph: "Automation could displace workers."
Threat: YES (arguably) - job displacement scenario presented
Benefit: NO/UNCLEAR - "could" creates hedging; decide: is this framed as threat?
Note: Hard case - decision rule needed for when speculative displacement = threat frame
```

---

## STEP-BY-STEP ANNOTATION PROCESS

1. **Read paragraph carefully** – Note any economic language
2. **Identify threat indicators** – Circle/mark threat language
3. **Identify benefit indicators** – Circle/mark benefit language
4. **Make THREAT decision** – YES or NO based on threat framing
5. **Write THREAT justification** – Explain both why YES/NO and why NOT (opposite)
6. **Make BENEFIT decision** – YES or NO based on benefit framing
7. **Write BENEFIT justification** – Explain both why YES/NO and why NOT (opposite)
8. **Check combination** – Mark which pattern (threat only, benefit only, both, neither)
9. **Rate difficulty** – Flag hard cases for discussion
10. **Document concerns** – Note any ambiguities or needed clarifications
